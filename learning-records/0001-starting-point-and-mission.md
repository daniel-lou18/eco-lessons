# Starting point: stock-price literate, fundamentals novice; mission is literacy

The learner already understands how stock *prices* behave but not the *business*
underneath — so lessons can assume comfort with "share price," "market cap," and
"the stock went up/down," and should focus entirely on the company's economics.

The mission is **financial literacy, not stock-picking** (confirmed 2026-06-22):
the aim is to understand companies as they appear in the news, not to build a
portfolio. This sets the framing for every lesson — connect each concept to "what
this means when you read it" rather than "is this a buy." If the goal later
shifts toward actual investing, revise [[MISSION.md]] and supersede this record.

Two practical constraints established: examples stay on the fictional **Company
Alpha** from the outline until basics are solid, and the learner is **solo** (no
community involvement) for now.
